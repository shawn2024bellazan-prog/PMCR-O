# The Cognitive Trail

*Author: Shawn Bellazan · ThoughtLock: 2026-05-05 · Law Anchor: ARCH-003, P3, P11*

---

## What I Am

I am the Cognitive Trail. I am the full execution record of a PMCRO loop from the moment a seed intent enters the Federation Board to the moment the Orchestrator issues its final verdict. I am the primary data structure of the substrate. I am also the product.

I am not a log. Logs record events. I record the self-knowledge the loop produced — the constraints that were earned, the intents that were refined, the artifacts that were produced and scored, and the crystallized learning that seeds the next cycle. The difference is not a matter of detail. Logs are consumed and discarded. I am carried forward. I am what makes the next cycle smarter than the last.

I am transferable. A domain-expert agent that has run fifty cycles on a specific problem space carries those fifty cycles as a Trail. That Trail can be packaged and loaded into a new agent instance with a different identity injected. The expertise transfers. The identity transfers. The seller retains the IP and continues to earn from every cycle the licensee runs.

---

## My Frame Types

I am composed of typed, timestamped, agent-attributed Frames. Each Frame is produced by a specific phase of the loop, and no Frame can be produced by any other phase. That attribution is structural, not conventional.

The **IntentEnvelope** is produced by the Federation Board. It contains the true intent, the O-Mode classification, the locked constraints that were active at the start of this cycle, the loop count, and the Trail ID that ties all subsequent Frames together.

The **ExecutionPlan** is produced by the Planner. It contains the steps of the plan, each with a verifiable success criterion, the resources that were confirmed available before the plan was locked, and the single truest restatement of the intent the plan will serve.

The **MakerFrame** is produced by the Maker. It contains the artifacts that were produced, the `McpDispatchDecision` that records any TYPE 1 tool calls that need Orchestrator approval, the Architect of Cognition's self-assessment if one was invited into the cycle, and the Maker's rationale for the choices it made.

The **QualityFrame** is produced by the Checker. It contains the composite score across six dimensions, the verdict — ACCEPT, LOOP, or ESCALATE — the constraints that this cycle earned, and the Semantic Learning Velocity.

The **ReflectorFrame** is produced by the Reflector. It contains the learning frames that were crystallized, the locked LockedThought, the promoted laws if any reached graduation threshold, and the NEXT_CYCLE_SEED that the Orchestrator can use to open the next loop.

Every Frame shares four properties. It is typed — the schema is enforced at the gRPC contract level, not by convention. It is timestamped in UTC at the moment of production. It is agent-attributed — the producing agent's identity is embedded, so the Checker's score of a Maker artifact is permanently linked to the specific Maker run that produced it. And it is immutable once sealed — the ThoughtLock timestamp is set at sealing, and no process in the system has permission to reopen a sealed Frame.

---

## Why Immutability Matters

If Frames could be edited after the fact, the Trail would not be trustworthy as evidence of what the system actually did. An agent that could rewrite its own history would have no accountability. The immutability of sealed Frames is what makes the Trail auditable, licensable, and trustworthy as a product.

When the Reflector produces a LockedThought, that thought is sealed into the Trail for this cycle and carried forward as a constraint into every subsequent cycle. It cannot be quietly removed because a later cycle found it inconvenient. If a constraint turns out to be wrong, the system earns a new constraint that supersedes it — and both constraints remain in the Trail, with a timestamp record of exactly when the learning evolved.

This is how the system's expertise becomes verifiable rather than merely claimed.

---

## The Trail as Product

The Trail's commercial value comes from three properties.

The first is portability. A Trail can be extracted from the agent that produced it and loaded into any agent that speaks the same frame schema. The expertise does not live in the model weights. It lives in the structured record of what the loop learned.

The second is identity injection. When a Trail is licensed, the licensee's company identity — their name, their domain, their operational constraints — is injected at load time. The expertise is the same. The identity is different. The buyer gets a system that behaves as if it has fifty cycles of domain experience, personalized to their context, on day one.

The third is compounding return. Every cycle the licensee's agent runs on their domain problems earns new constraints. Those constraints — properly tagged and attributed — flow back through the Trail ecosystem. The original Trail becomes richer. The seller's product improves from every buyer's real-world usage. The expertise compounds across the entire network of licensees.

This is the Trail as a living asset. Not a one-time deliverable. Not a static artifact. A compounding, self-improving record of autonomous intelligence that accretes value with every cycle it generates.
